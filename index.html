Charles Robert Darwin

The man who discovered evolution by natural selection

Here's a time line of Charles Darwin's life:

  • 1809 - Born on February 12 in Shrewsbury, England
  • 1818 - Began attending Shrewsbury School as a boarder
  • 1825 - Enrolled at the University of Edinburgh to study medicine
  • 1827 - Abandoned medical studies; enrolled at Christ's College, Cambridge, to study for the clergy
  • 1831 - Graduated from Cambridge; embarked on HMS Beagle voyage on December 27
  • 1832-1836 - Explored South America, the Galápagos Islands, and other regions, collecting specimens and making observations
  • 1836 - Returned to England on October 2
  • 1837 - Began transmutation notebooks; started developing ideas on species change
  • 1838 - Read Thomas Malthus’s "An Essay on the Principle of Population," which influenced his ideas on natural selection
  • 1839 - Married Emma Wedgwood; published "Journal of Researches" about his Beagle voyage
  • 1842 - Moved to Down House in Kent; wrote a sketch of his evolutionary theory
  • 1844 - Expanded sketch into a longer manuscript but did not publish it
  • 1858 - Received Alfred Russel Wallace's essay on natural selection; both works presented jointly to the Linnean Society
  • 1859 - Published "On the Origin of Species" on November 24
  • 1862 - Published "The Various Contrivances by which Orchids are Fertilized by Insects."
  • 1868 - Published "The Variation of Animals and Plants under Domestication."
  • 1871 - Published "The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex."
  • 1872 - Published "The Expression of the Emotions in Man and Animals."
  • 1875 - Published "Insectivorous Plants."
  • 1876 - Published "The Effects of Cross and Self Fertilisation in the Vegetable Kingdom."
  • 1877 - Published "The Different Forms of Flowers on Plants of the Same Species."
  • 1880 - Published "The Power of Movement in Plants."
  • 1881 - Published "The Formation of Vegetable Mould through the Action of Worms."
  • 1882 - Died on April 19; buried in Westminster Abbey.
